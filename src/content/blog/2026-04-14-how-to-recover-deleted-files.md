Believe it or not, tackling the issue of how to recover deleted files is often simpler than people anticipate. By following a few targeted steps, you can bring an old computer back to life without having to think about immediate replacement. We’ve skipped the confusing technical jargon and focused on practical advice you can implement today. If your goal is simply to speed up an old PC, start with the free fixes listed first. Then, use the later sections to gather the information needed to decide if shelling out cash for an SSD, more RAM, or a full replacement is truly worthwhile. You will have a clear answer before you finish reading.

## Quick Answer

**Short version:** If you need to know how to speed up an old PC, you can likely handle the majority of the fixes yourself in under an hour — and you won't need special tools. Work through the steps below in this order, starting with the easiest remedies.

## What You'll Need

Before you begin, gather these items:

- About 20–30 minutes of uninterrupted time
- Access to your device's settings or admin panel
- A notepad or phone to jot down anything unusual you notice during the process
- A device (computer, laptop, or phone) connected to your home network


## Step 1: Start With the Basics

Let's start with the easiest wins. This initial step helps determine if the slow performance is due to simple clutter rather than a hardware failure.

1. Open the relevant settings panel on your device — usually located within the main system menu.
2. Look for any settings related to how to recover deleted files and check whether any setting appears incorrect.
3. Restart the relevant app or service after making the necessary changes.
4. Test the result before you move on to the next step.

## Step 2: Dig a Little Deeper

If Step 1 only provided minimal improvement, this stage helps determine if the issue is related to software bloat, malware, or simply insufficient free disk space.

1. Download and install any pending system updates — applying these updates often fixes many underlying issues.
2. Check carefully for any error messages or alerts, and be sure to note them down before doing anything else.
3. Run a quick diagnostic or built-in scan if one is available for restore deleted photos.
4. Reboot your device completely after finishing this step; don't just let it sleep or wake up.


## Step 3: Advanced Fixes

Only proceed to this stage if the free fixes listed above haven't been sufficient—these changes are the most likely indicators of whether the PC is worth keeping.

1. At this point, we are attempting to address the underlying cause, not just the symptoms.
2. Make sure to back up any important data before making any of the changes listed below.
3. Follow each sub-step meticulously — remember that these changes are often reversible if you need to undo them.
4. If these steps successfully resolve the problem, jot down a note for your own future reference.

## Troubleshooting Tips

Still running into trouble? Consider trying these few additional things:

- Document everything you've already attempted before calling technical support — this speeds up the diagnosis process dramatically.
- Sometimes, a recent software update causes unforeseen problems. Search online to see if other users are reporting the same issue.
- If you're still stuck after exhausting all the steps, perform a full device restart—not just putting it in sleep mode.
- Check the manufacturer's official support page or community forums for issues specific to your device model.

## Wrapping Up

To summarize the crucial takeaways:

- Match how to recover deleted files to how you will genuinely use the computer, rather than focusing only on the most impressive spec sheet.
- Prioritize the option that eliminates the biggest day-to-day frustration.
- When faced with two choices that seem equally compelling, the simpler one is usually the better bet for long-term comfort.

If you only have 15 minutes to work with, start by checking startup apps, free disk space, and doing a quick restart before considering any monetary investments.
