Learning how to fix a computer that won't turn on is often simpler than people imagine; you just need a solid sequence of steps. If your PC takes forever to boot, stutters while running basic tabs, or sounds like it's struggling for breath, start here before considering expensive purchases. This guide covers the fundamentals first, followed by deeper details that truly inform your decision. If you are looking to know how to speed up an old PC, start by implementing the free fixes, and then use the later sections to determine if an SSD, more RAM, or full replacement is genuinely worth the effort. Bookmark this article—it's the complete resource you'll want to keep handy.

## Quick Answer

**TL;DR:** Always start with the quick, software-side fixes before touching any hardware. In most scenarios, this entire process can be resolved in 20–30 minutes without spending a dime, which is why understanding how to speed up an old PC usually begins with cleanup rather than costly upgrades.

**Quick keyword recap:** For readers comparing how to fix a computer that wont turn on, the practical answer depends on your real setup and budget.

## What You'll Need

Before beginning, gather these items:

- Any login or account details you might need during troubleshooting
- A notepad or phone to jot down anything unusual you observe during the process
- A device (computer, laptop, or phone) connected to your home network
- About 20–30 minutes of uninterrupted time


## Step 1: Start With the Basics

The fastest wins often come from simple adjustments—this step helps determine if the slowdown is mostly caused by clutter or by failing hardware.

1. Open the relevant settings panel on your device; this is usually located within the main system menu.
2. Look specifically for any settings related to "PC not turning on" and check if any setting appears incorrect.
3. After making changes, restart the relevant application or service.
4. Test the resulting function before proceeding to the next step.

## Step 2: Dig a Little Deeper

If the initial basic fixes only yielded minor improvements, this step helps determine if the core issue lies with software load, malware, or low available disk space.

1. Download and install all pending system updates—many issues are resolved through updates that haven't been applied yet.
2. Check for any error messages or alerts, and write them down before changing anything else.
3. Run a quick built-in scan or diagnostic tool if one is available for identifying why the PC is not turning on.
4. After completing this step, perform a full reboot of your device, rather than just using sleep or wake mode.


## Step 3: Advanced Fixes

Only proceed to this stage if the free fixes were insufficient—these are the changes most likely to tell you if the PC is worth keeping.

1. At this stage, the goal is to address the root cause, not just the noticeable symptoms.
2. Before making the changes below, ensure you back up any important data.
3. Follow every sub-step carefully; remember, these changes can usually be reversed if needed.
4. If this process resolves the problem, make a detailed note of the steps taken for future reference.

## Troubleshooting Tips

Still running into issues? Try these additional things:

- If you're stuck after running all previous steps, try a complete device restart—do not just use sleep mode.
- Always document what troubleshooting steps you've already taken before calling support; this significantly speeds up diagnosis.
- Contact device or service support if the issue persists—some critical fixes require system access you may not have locally.
- When in doubt, a factory reset (used only as a final measure) resolves most stubborn, persistent problems.

## Wrapping Up

To summarize the decision process:

- Base how to fix a computer that won't turn on on how you plan to use it day-to-day, rather than focusing on the most impressive spec sheet.
- Prioritize the option that removes the biggest, most frequent real-world annoyance.
- When two choices seem equally viable, the simpler option is typically easier to maintain long term.

If two or three quick fixes don't significantly change your daily usage, stop guessing blindly and compare the cost of an SSD or RAM upgrade against the cost of replacing the PC entirely.

In short, how to fix a computer that wont turn on is easiest to judge when you focus on the everyday use case first.
